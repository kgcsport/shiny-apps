# Auction Game

## Teaching Goal

Help students explore bidding, valuation, surplus, market clearing, or winner's curse behavior.

## Classroom Flow

The instructor assigns fake valuations, opens bidding, closes the auction, reveals winners and prices, and exports bids.

## Student Actions

Students submit bids, revise bids if allowed, or choose whether to participate.

## Instructor Actions

Choose auction format, set item supply, assign values, open and close bidding, reveal outcomes, and export data.

## Data Saved

Timestamp, session ID, round number, participant ID, value, bid, auction format, allocation, price, and surplus.

## Possible Scoring Or Payoff Logic

In a first-price auction, winner payoff equals value minus own bid. In a second-price auction, winner payoff equals value minus the second-highest bid.

## What To Test

Test ties, missing bids, invalid bids, repeated rounds, price calculation, and whether private values stay hidden.

## Possible Extensions

Add sealed-bid auctions, English auctions, procurement auctions, common values, or treatment comparisons.
